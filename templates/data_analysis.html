GDP Per Capita Dataset

Our dataset contains the average GDP per capita from 1970 to 2017 for 227 countries as well as the average of other metrics, such as Infant mortality (per 1000 births) and Literacy %, for each country.

We want our model to be able to predict the GDP per capita for a country based on inputted values for the other metrics.

Here is a link to our dataset: GDP Per Capita Dataset

Here are some details on our less interpretable features:

  • Arable %: Percentage of land capable of cultivating crops
  • Crop %: Percentage of arable land that is actually under cultivation and used for growing crops
  • Agriculture: Percentage of GDP that comes from agriculture
  • Industry: Percentage of GDP that comes from industrial activities
  • Service: Percentage of GDP from the service industry
Some of the metrics for a country are null, so we filled in these values with the median metric value of the region the country is in.

Graphs Heatmap

Above is a heatmap of the correlations between the 10 most impactful features, according to our Random Forest model feature selection, and the target, GDP per capita. As seen in the color scale to the right, yellow indicates that there is a strong positive correlation between the two variables, red indicates that there is weak or no correlation, and dark purple indicates a strong negative correlation.

Some interesting observations are that Phones (per 1000) and GDP per capita have a strong positive correlation, while Birthrate and GDP per capita have a moderate to strong negative correlation.
